The Regular Editorial Team

Who writes the Regular blog — and how we keep it honest. Short version: we're founders and parents, not therapists, and everything we publish is grounded in published research with the sources right there to check.

How we write

  • We're not licensed therapists. We don't give clinical or medical advice, and we say so. For anything serious — postpartum depression, abuse, crisis — we point you to professionals and helplines.
  • Every claim has a source. We ground articles in established relationship science — John Gottman's research, Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT), attachment research — and cite peer-reviewed studies and bodies like Postpartum Support International, with links. No invented statistics.
  • We write from real life. Most of us are parents who've been through the post-baby year ourselves. That lived experience — not a fake credential — is what we bring, in plain, judgment-free language for dads.
  • No fake authority. We sign articles with our real names and real roles. We'll never invent a "Dr." or a therapist title we don't hold.
Clinical review. We're bringing on a licensed therapist (LMFT) as an editorial advisor. As soon as that's in place, sensitive articles will carry a "Reviewed by [name], LMFT" line. Until then, we're transparent that review is editorial, not clinical.
